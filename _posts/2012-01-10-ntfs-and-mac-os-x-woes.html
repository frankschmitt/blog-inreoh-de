--- layout: post title: NTFS and Mac OS X woes date: '2012-01-10T12:29:00.000-08:00' author: Frank Schmitt tags: - NTFS-3G MacFuse MacOSX NTFS modified_time: '2012-07-23T13:18:34.944-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6373343020354235841.post-410589134218718450 blogger_orig_url: http://inreoh.blogspot.com/2012/01/ntfs-and-mac-os-x-woes.html --- Every once in a while, when trying to mount a NTFS drive on Mac OS X with NTFS-3G + MacFuse, I get an error stating "NTFS-3G could not mount - unclean shutdown".

Contrary to the message box that pops up, mount -o force .. doesn't help; what helps is:

sudo mkdir /Volumes/NTFS

sudo /usr/local/bin/ntfs-3g -o force /dev/rdisk2s1 /Volumes/NTFS/

This will create a directory /Volumes/NTFS and mount the NTFS drive there (assuming /dev/rdisk2s1 is the correct device, of course).
